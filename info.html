Information and fun facts about Formula 1

Information :


Grand Prix motor racing, a form of motorsport competition, in fact, it is the most important and difficult competition for any F1 Driver, and winning them is not a easy task.
Lewis Hamilton holds the record for the most race wins in Formula One history, with 103 wins to date. Michael Schumacher, the previous record holder, is second with 91 wins, and Sebastian Vettel is third with 53 victories.
These 5 pilots have reached the podium many times over the course of the years and are the more reconised faces of F1.

The major event of any competition is the final, Formula 1 is no different. Every year since 1950, one driver takes the first place in the podium and is considered the best pilot of that same year.
Some of this drivers have won multiple times, for example, Michael Schumacher and Lewis Hamilton have a sum of 7 wins, while others, like Max Verstappen (the winner of 2021's championship), have managed to only win one in their whole career. This graph shows the percentage of how many pilots have won championships per number of occurance.
Only two drivers have won this prize 7 times and in total 17 drivers have won 1 time only.

Over the years many drivers with differtent nationalities have entered the world of F1.
In total over 40 (43 to be precise) nationalities have been represented in F1, some more than others.
This graph shows the number of Drivers by country, the more green it is, the more Dirvers the country has that represented, or still represent them during races.
The country with the most quantity is Great Britain with a total of 165, followed by The United States with a total of 157.
Other countries such as the Czech Republic only had one Driver in their history.

Fun Facts :


Number 1 An f1 car can reach up to 360 kmph during a race.
Number 2 Despite the massive amount of heat in the cockpit, an f1 driver has to wear two suits. One protects him from injuries, and one protects him from the heat.
Number 3 Some of the best f1 pit crews can refuel and change the tires in just three seconds.
Number 4 An f1 car weighs around 728 kg without fuel (driver included)!
Number 5 During a race, an f1 driver loses up to 4 kg of body weight due to the unbearable temperature in the cockpit.
Number 6 An F1 car can (theoretically) drive upside down for two seconds, but nobody has ever done it till date!
Number 7 F1 is called 'formula' one because drivers have to follow around 500 rules and regulations (hence - formulas) before participating in a race.
Number 8 During a run, the cockpit can heat up to 50 degree Celsius.
Number 9 F1 helmets are so strong and durable that they can last temperature of 800-degree Celsius for 45 seconds.
Number 10 An F1 car can (theoretically) accelerate from 0 to 100 kmph in just one second. However, the practical time to do so is two seconds!
